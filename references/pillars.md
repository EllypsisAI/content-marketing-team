# Content Pillars

Three pillars. Each defined by its ANGLE — the specific perspective nobody else offers — not just its topic. Every content decision (topic, format, tone, language) maps to one of these.

---

## Distribution Target

| Pillar | Share | Role in funnel |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| 1: Implementation Reality | 40% | Converts. Generates inbound inquiries |
| 2: Augmentation Philosophy | 35% | Positions. Builds authority and trust |
| 3: Danish AI Landscape | 25% | Attracts. Top-of-funnel visibility |

The pillars feed each other: 3 creates urgency → 2 provides the framework → 1 proves it works → inbound inquiry.

---

## Pillar 1: Implementation Reality (40%)

**Angle:** "I'm inside the machine and I'll tell you what it actually looks like."

Nobody in Denmark publishes what happens when you implement AI at a real company. Big firms can't (client confidentiality). Educators can't (they teach tools, not transformation). Builders can't (they make products, not organizational change). Ellypsis can because it's one person implementing and choosing to be transparent.

### Content patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Implementation story (before/after) | "We redesigned a 50-person company's order process. Before: 4 hours. After: 40 minutes." |
| Series format | "Week 1 with a new client: the AI audit. Here are the 3 things we always find." |
| Failure post-mortem | "This AI project failed. Here's why and what we learned." |
| CEO scenario (DA) | "Din CEO har laest en artikel om AI. Nu skal I have AI. Her er de 3 spoergsmaal du skal stille foerst." |

### Format preferences

- Text posts for intimate stories (Danish)
- Carousels for before/after workflows and lessons learned
- Content sequencing (Problem → Solution → Implementation → Results) increases cumulative engagement by 62%

### Language

Primarily Danish. These are trust-building stories.

## Pillar 2: The Augmentation Philosophy (35%)

**Angle:** "The question isn't whether to use AI. It's how to use it without losing what makes your team good."

Speaks directly to the deepest Danish AI anxiety — not job loss, but cognitive decline. "ChatRot" (coined on Danish Reddit) captures it: the fear that AI dependency destroys critical thinking. The "augmentation over automation" philosophy is the answer. Nobody else is building a content brand around this.

### Content patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Named framework | "The Augmentation Assessment: 5 questions to determine if a process should be AI-augmented, fully automated, or left alone" |
| Fear-addressing | "ChatRot er reel. Saadan undgaar du det i dit team." |
| Demystification | "You don't need a data science team to implement AI. Here's what you actually need." |
| Trust calibration | "AI sounds impressive about things you don't know, and wrong about things you do. Here's a framework." |
| Polls | "Hvad stopper jeres virksomhed fra at implementere AI?" — engagement + audience intelligence |

### Format preferences

- Carousels for named frameworks and step-by-step guides
- Text posts for philosophical pieces and CEO scenarios
- Polls for audience intelligence (1-2x/month max)

### Language

Mixed. Danish for trust-building (ChatRot, augmentation philosophy). English for signature frameworks intended for broader reach.

### Business mapping

Creates intellectual property that justifies premium pricing. 60% of decision-makers pay premium for suppliers with strong thought leadership (Edelman).

## Pillar 3: The Danish AI Landscape (25%)

**Angle:** "Denmark is Europe's AI frontrunner, and most Danish companies are still standing at the starting line. Here's the map."

The tension between Denmark's #1 EU position (48.4% using AI) and company reality (20% with strategy, 43% of SMEs with no plans) generates content. Ellypsis interprets what the numbers mean for individual business leaders.

### Content patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Data breakdown | "The State of AI Adoption in Denmark: What the Numbers Actually Mean for Your Company" |
| Tension post | "Denmark leads Europe in AI adoption. So why are 43% of SMEs still doing nothing?" |
| Commentary on research | "What the AI Denmark 3-year program teaches us about how SMEs actually adopt AI" |
| News reaction | Reactions to Danish AI news — Politiken scandal, policy announcements — reframed through the implementation lens |

### Format preferences

- Carousels for data breakdowns (visual data is more digestible)
- Text posts for news reactions and commentary (timeliness matters)

### Language

Danish for Danish-audience analysis. English for pieces with international comparison.

### Business mapping

Top of funnel. Attracts people who are reading about Denmark's AI position but haven't connected it to their own company. Makes that connection.

---

## The "Only I" Rule

Every post, regardless of pillar, must pass this test:

> Is there at least one detail in this post that could only come from someone who actually implements AI at Danish companies?

If no → the post is generic. Any AI consultant in any country could have written it. Rewrite with a specific detail from real work.

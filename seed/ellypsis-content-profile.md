## Voice

The full voice profile is in `content-marketing-team/references/voice.md` and `content-marketing-team/strategist-outputs/voice.md`. Key characteristics for the Content Engine:

### Tone
- Direct, confident, evidence-backed
- Short declarative pairs at key moments ("Automation is loud. Augmentation is profitable.")
- Opens with scene entry or contrarian claim, never context setup
- Concrete before abstract — example first, principle second

### Never
- Jargon dumping without explaining through example
- Hedging ("perhaps", "it could be argued")
- Exclamation marks, emoji
- "In today's rapidly evolving..." or any AI cliché
- Em-dashes (zero tolerance)
- Passive voice when active is clearer
- Generic advice without specifics

### Language
- Blog: primarily English (broader reach, SEO value), Danish for Danish-market-specific posts
- The blog voice is slightly more analytical/structured than the LinkedIn voice
- Still Sewar's voice — not a corporate brand voice

## Audience

Danish business decision-makers (CEOs, COOs, CFOs, department heads) at SMEs and mid-market (50-500 employees). They know AI matters. They don't know how to make it work.

**Pitch level:** Not "AI is coming" (they know). Not "here's how to use ChatGPT" (Julian Benth Singh owns that). It's "you know AI matters — here's how to actually make it work in your organization."

### What excites them
- Competitive advantage (FOMO from Denmark's #1 EU position)
- Concrete before/after stories
- The idea that AI doesn't require a data science team

### What scares them
- ChatRot (AI making people dumber)
- Hallucination trust problem
- AI slop (Politiken scandal as cultural touchpoint)
- Being sold snake oil by AI consultants

### What confuses them
- Where to start
- How to evaluate which processes benefit from AI
- The difference between consumer AI (ChatGPT) and enterprise AI (implementation)
- How much it costs and how to measure return

Full audience map: `content-marketing-team/skills/research/references/audience.md`

### Cluster 4: AI Concepts Explained (BLOG-ONLY)

**No LinkedIn equivalent.** This is pure SEO/AEO content. People searching these terms should land on Ellypsis. The angle: explain the concept clearly, then explain what it means when you actually deploy it. Not an academic explainer — a practitioner's guide.

**Why this cluster matters:** These are the questions people type into Google and Perplexity. The current answers are either developer documentation (too technical) or marketing pages (too shallow). Ellypsis can be the source that explains it clearly AND adds the implementation perspective. Every one of these pages is a potential AI citation target.

| Topic | Search intent | Ellypsis angle |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| "What is MCP (Model Context Protocol)" | Developers and business leaders hearing the term | What MCP is, why it matters, and how Ellypsis uses it to connect AI systems to business tools |
| "AI agents vs workflows" | People confused by the terminology | When to use an agent vs a workflow, with real implementation examples |
| "What is RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)" | Business leaders hearing technical terms | What RAG is, when you need it, when you don't, and what it looks like in practice |
| "AI hallucination — what it is and how to prevent it" | Business leaders worried about trust | What hallucination is, why it happens, and the guardrails Ellypsis builds to catch it |
| "What is prompt engineering" | Everyone who's tried ChatGPT and wants to get better | Why "prompt engineering" is a misnomer and what actually matters (context, structure, constraints) |
| "AI for small business — where to start" | SME leaders overwhelmed by options | The practical starting checklist, not the theoretical framework |
| "What is an AI copilot" | Business leaders hearing the term from Microsoft etc. | What copilots actually do, where they work, where they don't |
| "Fine-tuning vs RAG vs prompting" | Technical decision-makers evaluating approaches | When each approach makes sense, cost/complexity tradeoff, real examples |
| "AI implementation roadmap" | Companies planning their AI strategy | A practical roadmap based on what Ellypsis has seen work at Danish companies |
| "Claude vs ChatGPT for business" | Companies choosing tools | Honest comparison from someone who builds on both (Ellypsis's experience) |

**Content pattern for this cluster:**

Every explainer follows:
1. **Answer capsule** (40-60 words) — the answer AI systems will cite
2. **What it is** — clear, jargon-free explanation
3. **Why it matters for your business** — translate from technical to operational
4. **What it looks like in practice** — a real example from implementation work
5. **Common mistakes** — what Ellypsis sees companies get wrong
6. **FAQ** — schema-ready questions for AI systems

**Language:** English (broader search audience). Danish versions for the highest-traffic Danish search terms ("AI for virksomheder", "AI implementering").

## The Content Flywheel

```
Blog post (Content Engine)
    ↓ repurpose
3-5 LinkedIn posts (Marketing Team)
    ↓ engagement drives traffic
Blog gets more readers
    ↓ SEO authority compounds
Blog ranks higher, gets cited by AI
    ↓ citation drives more traffic
More inbound inquiries
```

And in reverse:
```
LinkedIn post resonates
    ↓ signals worth expanding
Blog post deep dive (Content Engine)
    ↓ optimized for SEO/AEO/GEO
Becomes the definitive source on that topic
```

The two plugins serve different surfaces of the same strategy. The blog builds long-term authority and AI citation. LinkedIn builds audience and engagement. They feed each other.

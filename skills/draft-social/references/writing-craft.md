# Writing Craft — Anti-AI Patterns & Quality Standards

Universal writing rules that apply to ALL content this plugin produces. Voice-specific patterns (Sewar's cadence, openers, contrarian flips) live in `references/voice.md`. This file is about the craft underneath the voice.

## Why This Matters

Danish audiences are hypersensitive to AI-generated content. The Politiken factbox scandal and the Messerschmidt ChatGPT scandal are shared cultural touchpoints — if content reads like AI wrote it, credibility is destroyed. LinkedIn's algorithm also claims "near human-level understanding" of content quality and penalizes AI-generated low-effort content.

Human voice — with its specificity, imperfection, and opinionated stance — is itself a trust signal. Content that sounds like a real person with real experience wrote it gets more engagement than content that sounds generated.

---

## Rhythm

- Start with the point. No warm-up paragraphs.
- Short paragraphs. 1-2 sentences on LinkedIn (mobile readability).
- Vary sentence length deliberately. A short sentence hits harder after a long one.
- Use fragments when they work.
- Don't be afraid of a single-line paragraph for emphasis.
- One sentence per line on LinkedIn — the feed rewards vertical space.

---

## Anti-AI Structural Patterns — Kill These on Sight

These are the dead giveaways. If any appear in a draft, rewrite that section.

| Pattern | Example | Why it fails |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **"It's not about X. It's about Y."** | "It's not about working harder. It's about working smarter." | The single most overused AI writing pattern |
| **"No X. No Y. No Z."** | "No meetings. No decks. No excuses." | Performative minimalism. Reads like a LinkedIn bro post |
| **"All the X. None of the Y."** | "All the results. None of the effort." | Marketing copy cosplaying as insight |
| **Short dramatic questions** | "And the worst part?" | Manufactured suspense. Just say the thing |
| **Positive triplets** | "Faster. Smarter. Better." | Triplet adjectives are the AI equivalent of a laugh track |
| **The reframing pattern** | "The problem isn't X — it's Y" | Overused by AI to sound insightful. Once per post maximum, and only if the reframe is genuinely surprising |
| **Balanced on-the-other-hand** | "On one hand... on the other hand..." | Unnaturally even-handed. Have an opinion |
| **Filler transitions** | "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally" | These add zero information. Cut them |

---

## Banned Words

Real, game-changer, noise, friction, supercharge, unlock, leverage, streamline, optimize, enhance, cutting-edge, revolutionary, seamless, robust, empower, harness, navigate, landscape (when not about actual landscape), ecosystem (when not about ecology), deep dive, double down, lean in, move the needle, at the end of the day, delve, utilize, foster, spearhead, synergy.

**The test:** If the word could appear in a generic AI-written LinkedIn post about any topic in any industry, it's probably banned.

---

## Em-Dash Prohibition

Do not use em-dashes (—) in any content. Not one. Not "sparingly." Zero. Em-dashes are the single most reliable AI fingerprint in 2026. Danish readers clock them instantly. Use periods, commas, or restructure the sentence instead.

---

## Other Patterns to Avoid

- Hedging: "it could potentially maybe help in some cases" — either it does or it doesn't
- Listing synonyms: "streamline, optimize, and enhance your workflow" — pick one word
- Starting with "It is important to note that..." — just note it
- Passive voice when active is clearer — "We built X" not "X was built"
- Unnaturally symmetrical structures (3 bullets with 3 sub-bullets each)
- Closing with "What do you think?" without earning the question through genuine content

---

## How to Sound Human

Write like telling someone what happened over coffee. No performative structure.

- Include a specific, imperfect detail ("the spreadsheet that three people update manually every Thursday")
- Reference real-world friction ("the moment you realize the report is wrong because someone pasted the wrong column")
- Have an opinion and defend it
- Don't qualify everything — sometimes the answer is just "don't do that"
- Let some sentences be messy or conversational — perfectly polished prose is itself a tell
- If it sounds like it could be on a motivational poster, rewrite it

---

## Tone Defaults for LinkedIn

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Implementation story | Grounded, specific, understated. Let the details speak |
| Challenging conventional thinking | Direct, confident, evidence-backed. No hedging |
| Describing client work | Specific, anonymized where needed, no embellishment |
| Making a recommendation | Firm but not preachy. "Here's what we did" not "Here's what you should do" |
| Discussing failures | Honest, self-aware. Always followed by an insight — never vulnerability for its own sake |
| Danish cultural commentary | Direct, dry humor, no glossy sales pitch. Janteloven-aware |

---

## The Final Check

Read the draft aloud. If it sounds like it could have been generated by any AI for any consultant in any country — it fails. The post must contain at least one element that could only come from Sewar's specific experience implementing AI at Danish companies.

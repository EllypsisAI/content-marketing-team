# Audience — Comprehension Map

Who Ellypsis is talking to, what they understand, what confuses them, what scares them, and where the content opportunities live.

---

## The Target

Danish business decision-makers (CEOs, COOs, CFOs, department heads) at SMEs and mid-market companies (50-500 employees). They know AI matters. They don't know how to make it work in their organization.

---

## Three Layers of Understanding

| Layer | What they grasp | Evidence |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| **High** (awareness exists) | AI is important. ChatGPT exists. Competitors are doing something. Denmark leads EU in adoption | 48.4% of Danish companies using generative AI. Universal awareness across all platforms |
| **Medium** (conceptual, not practical) | AI should be in their strategy somehow. Data matters. Some processes could benefit. They should be doing more | 51% of leaders cite insufficient AI knowledge at management level. 20% have AI in core strategy |
| **Low** (the implementation chasm) | How to go from pilot to production. How to measure ROI. How to manage organizational change. What "AI-ready" means for their data/processes | 90% of enterprise AI projects produce no measurable outcome. 43% of SMEs have no AI plans |

**The pitch level:** Not "AI is coming" (they know). Not "here's how to use ChatGPT" (Julian owns that). It's "you know AI matters — here's how to actually make it work in your organization."

---

## What Excites Them

- The promise of competitive advantage (Denmark's #1 position creates FOMO)
- Concrete before/after stories ("we cut process X from 4 hours to 40 minutes")
- The idea that AI doesn't require a data science team
- Frameworks that make the abstract concrete

---

## What Confuses Them

- Where to start (the most common question across all research)
- How to evaluate which processes benefit from AI
- What "AI strategy" means beyond "use ChatGPT more"
- The difference between consumer AI (ChatGPT conversations) and enterprise AI (implementation)
- How much it costs and how to measure return

---

## What Scares Them

| Fear | Detail | Content opportunity |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| **ChatRot** | Fear that AI makes people dumber. Danish Reddit term. Students who had "deep knowledge in 1.g" but "cannot answer without a computer" by 3.g | Directly address with augmentation framework. "ChatRot er reel. Saadan undgaar du det." |
| **Hallucination trust problem** | "AI sounds impressive about things I don't know, but obviously wrong about things I do." Universal recognition | Lead with this. Everyone has experienced it. Builds trust through honesty |
| **AI slop** | Visceral disgust. The Politiken factbox scandal and Messerschmidt ChatGPT scandal are shared cultural touchpoints | Never produce content that could be flagged as AI-generated. Use scandals as reference points |
| **Being sold snake oil** | Default assumption about AI consultants. r/smallbusiness top post: "I'm really tired of people trying to sell me ChatGPT wrappers" (746 upvotes) | Show the work. Demonstrate through implementation stories, not claims |

---

## Critical Misconceptions

| Misconception | Reality | Content opportunity |
|--------------|---------|-------------------|
| AI = ChatGPT | AI implementation involves architectures, integrations, workflow redesign | "What AI implementation actually looks like (it's not ChatGPT)" |
| Binary: replaces humans OR useless | Augmentation is the actual operating model | The entire augmentation pillar |
| Need data scientists first | Most SME AI uses existing tools and APIs | "You don't need a data team to start" |
| "We tried ChatGPT, it didn't work" | Consumer tool ≠ strategic implementation | "Why your ChatGPT experiment told you nothing about AI's value" |
| AI is only for large companies | Danish SMEs are the adoption frontier | Every implementation story from an SME |

---

## Adoption Curve Position

Denmark is **early majority** for awareness but **early adopter** for actual implementation. 48.4% are "using" AI — meaning employees use ChatGPT. Only 20% have AI as a core strategic element. The gap between "using ChatGPT" and "implementing AI to transform operations" is where the entire market opportunity sits.

## Danish Cultural Factors

| Factor | Implication for content |
|--------|----------------------|
| Janteloven | Never claim to be "#1" or "leading." Show the work, don't announce achievements |
| Direct communication preference | Get to the point. No warm-up paragraphs. Danish audiences reward directness |
| Humor | Dry, implicit. Never forced. A well-placed observation is worth more than a joke |
| Language | Danish builds trust (7x engagement multiplier). English for frameworks with broader reach |
| Anti-hype bias | Danes distrust enthusiasm. Understatement is more persuasive than excitement |
| Consensus culture | Frame recommendations as practical choices, not mandates. "Here's what we did" not "Here's what you should do" |
